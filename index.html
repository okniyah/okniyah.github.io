History

In the 16th Century the region was conquered by the Spanish. Luckily, the Dutch revolted and became their own country. Their leader, Willem of Orange, formed the Republic of the United Netherlands in 1851. Over centuries, their power strengthened from growth of military and economy. It also weakened due to war. Especially during the 1900s. They were weakened during WWII. Many Jewish people were abducted from the Netherlands, includinng Anne Frank, a famous little girl who hid in the capital, Amsterdam. Today, the Netherlands is thriving and the people there are living peacefully.

Culture

The Netherlands is a very diverse country. It is filled with many different languages, religions, and they have a huge art culture. Their main spoken language is Dutch, but their are over 15+ languages that are spoken. Their are so many religions and surprisingly, there are many who are non religious or are atheist. Almost 50% are nonreligous. 23% are Roman Catholic. 15% are Protestant. 12% are other forms of religions. The Netherlands has one of the world's highest atheist population. The Netherlands are very familar with art. Some of the world's most famous pieces were made by Dutch artists. Some artists like Vincent van Gogh, Johannes Vermeer, and Adriaan de Lelie. They have many museums in the Netherlands to showcase their art as well as their architecture. The Netherlands are very culturally diverse.

Government

There are many forms of goverment. The Nethetherlands are a constitutional monarchy and a parliamentary democracy. This means they have a king as well as a prime minister. The king of the Netherlands is Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands. The prime minister of the netherlands is Mark Rutte. The king does not have actual political power but he is seen as head of state.
